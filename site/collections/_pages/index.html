My long-term aim is to grow broadly as an electrical engineer, then specialize in silicon engineering and chip design — the layer of the stack where physics and computation meet.

goals_title: Aspirations goals_html: >-

EE is a wide field, and I want to develop depth across it before narrowing in. That said, the direction I'm most drawn to is silicon engineering — understanding how transistors, analog circuits, and digital logic are physically realized on chip. Chip design sits at the intersection of physics, materials, and systems thinking, which is exactly the kind of multi-disciplinary challenge I thrive in.

To build toward that, I'm taking Electronic Devices and Circuit Technology at Georgia Tech — a course that dives into semiconductor physics, MOSFET operation, and device-level circuit behavior. I also plan to pursue a minor in Computer Science, since understanding the software and architectural layer that silicon serves makes me a stronger hardware engineer. The supercapacitor IC project I'm currently on is the most direct hands-on exposure I have to IC design so far, and I intend to keep threading in that direction.

Longer term, I want to be someone who can sit at the boundary of hardware and systems — fluent in both what the physics allows and what the application demands.
